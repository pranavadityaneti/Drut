2. What this policy covers

You can use this policy to raise any complaint about Drut. Complaints generally fall into two broad categories, and the same Grievance Officer handles both:

  • Data-protection grievances — anything about how your personal data is collected, used, shared, stored, corrected, or deleted, or about exercising your rights under India's data-protection law. See Section 3.
  • Consumer grievances — anything about your purchase, subscription, billing, cancellation, refunds, pricing, the service you received, or unfair-trade-practice concerns. See Section 4.

If your complaint touches both areas, you do not need to file twice — send it once and we will route it internally.

3. Data-protection grievances (DPDP Act / IT Rules)

As a Data Fiduciary under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 and a body corporate under the IT Act 2000 and SPDI Rules 2011 (both currently in force during the transition to the DPDP regime), we are required to provide an effective mechanism to redress grievances about your personal data. You have the right to grievance redressal (DPDP Act, Section 13).

You can raise a data-protection grievance about, for example:

  • Access — to obtain a summary of the personal data we hold about you and how we process it (DPDP Act, Section 11).
  • Correction, completion, or updating of your personal data (DPDP Act, Section 12).
  • Erasure / deletion of your personal data and account, where there is no legal reason for us to keep it (DPDP Act, Section 12).
  • Withdrawal of consent — and our duty to stop processing and erase where no other lawful ground applies (DPDP Act, Section 6).
  • Nomination — to nominate another person to exercise your rights in the event of your death or incapacity (DPDP Act, Section 14).
  • A concern that your data has been shared, lost, or exposed; or any other concern about how we (or our processors — Supabase, Google, and Razorpay) handle your data.

We must respond to and act on these requests and grievances within a defined timeframe — see Section 6. If you are not satisfied with how we resolve a data-protection grievance, you may escalate to the Data Protection Board of India — see Section 7.

4. Consumer grievances (Consumer Protection / E-Commerce Rules)

Drut Pro is a paid digital subscription sold directly by us to consumers, which makes us an "inventory e-commerce entity" (we sell our own service) under the Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules 2020. We are bound by the Consumer Protection Act 2019 and must provide a grievance-redressal channel and honour your statutory consumer rights.

You can raise a consumer grievance about, for example:

  • Billing, charges, or a payment processed through Razorpay.
  • Subscription, cancellation, or difficulty cancelling a Drut Pro subscription.
  • Refunds — including where you believe the service was defective, deficient, or not as described (the law requires a refund in such cases regardless of any "non-refundable" wording — see the flag below).
  • Pricing, taxes, or the figures shown to you before purchase.
  • The quality or delivery of the service, or any concern that we have engaged in an unfair trade practice, misleading claim, or "dark pattern".

Under Section 2(9) of the Consumer Protection Act 2019 you have all six statutory consumer rights, which we commit to honour:

5. How to raise a grievance

To help us resolve your complaint quickly, please include:

  • Your name and the email address registered with your Drut account.
  • Whether it is a data-protection or a consumer / billing grievance (if you are unsure, just describe the issue).
  • A clear description of the problem, with dates, screenshots, or a payment / transaction reference where relevant.
  • What outcome you are seeking (for example: correction of data, deletion, a refund, cancellation).

Send your grievance to the Grievance Officer at pranav@drut.club, or by post to the registered address in Section 1. You may also write to general support at support@drut.club and we will forward it to the Grievance Officer.

When we receive your grievance, we will assign it a complaint / ticket reference so you can track it through to resolution. (A trackable reference is recommended good practice for e-commerce grievances.)

6. Our response timelines

We follow one consistent standard for every grievance. Where more than one law applies to your complaint, we apply the one most favourable to you.

Stage Our commitment Legal basis
Acknowledge your complaint Within 48 hours of receipt (we aim to acknowledge sooner where we can), with a tracking / ticket reference. E-Commerce Rules 2020, Rule 4(5)
Resolve a consumer grievance Within one month (30 days) of receipt. E-Commerce Rules 2020, Rule 4(5)
Resolve a data-protection grievance / rights request As soon as possible, and in any case within the DPDP / SPDI statutory period — within one month where the SPDI Rules apply during the transition, and within the DPDP statutory period (reported as an outer limit of 90 days) once those provisions are in force. DPDP Act 2023, Section 13 & DPDP Rules 2025; SPDI Rules 2011, Rule 5(9)

In plain English: we reply within 2 days, and we fix consumer complaints within a month. Data complaints are handled within the period the law requires, and we will always try to be faster.

Note on the data-protection timeline: the precise grievance-redressal period is set by the DPDP Rules 2025 (notified 13 November 2025; substantive obligations commencing in phases up to around 13 May 2027). Commentary places the outer limit at 90 days. While the SPDI Rules 2011 remain in force during the transition, we apply the stricter one-month SPDI timeline to data grievances. The exact statutory period should be confirmed against the gazetted DPDP Rules by counsel before this document is published.

7. Escalation if you are not satisfied

If we do not resolve your grievance within the timelines above, or you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to escalate.

7.1 Data-protection grievances — Data Protection Board of India

For complaints about your personal data, you may lodge a complaint with the Data Protection Board of India in the manner prescribed under the DPDP Act 2023 and the DPDP Rules 2025. The Board is the statutory authority that hears data-protection complaints once the relevant provisions are in force. We will cooperate with any inquiry by the Board.

7.2 Consumer grievances — consumer forums and helpline

For consumer, billing, or service complaints, you may approach:

  • The National Consumer Helpline — dial 1915 or visit consumerhelpline.gov.in.
  • The appropriate Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (District, State, or National, depending on the value of the claim) under the Consumer Protection Act 2019.
  • The Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA), which acts on unfair trade practices, misleading advertisements, and dark patterns.

For payment-related complaints, you may also raise the issue with your bank or card issuer, or with Razorpay (our payment processor), in addition to contacting us.

8. Grievances about a minor's data

A large share of Drut's users are aged 16–18 and are minors (under 18) under Indian law. Drut may be used by a minor only with the verifiable consent of a parent or lawful guardian, and our Privacy Policy sets out the children's-data protections we apply under Section 9 of the DPDP Act.

A parent or lawful guardian may raise a grievance on a minor's behalf — including to review the minor's data, withdraw consent, or request deletion — using the same channels in Section 5. We may ask for reasonable verification that you are the child's parent or guardian before we act.

For users under 18, we do not behaviourally track or monitor, and we do not serve targeted advertising directed at children. If you believe a child's data has been handled in any way that does not match this commitment, please raise it with the Grievance Officer and we will treat it as a priority.

Default assumption to confirm [CONFIRM]: We assume minors may use Drut with verifiable parental/guardian consent, and we do not behaviourally track, profile, or serve targeted advertising to under-18 users. Whether an ed-tech self-study app qualifies for any "educational institution" exemption to the children's-consent rules is genuinely unsettled — we draft conservatively and assume the full verifiable-parental-consent regime applies. Confirm with Indian data-protection counsel before relying on any exemption.

9. Fair dealing and good faith

We will deal with every grievance fairly, in good faith, and without retaliation. We do not penalise you for raising a complaint or for exercising your rights. Using this grievance channel does not take away any right you have to approach the Data Protection Board, a consumer forum, or any other competent authority directly.
