1. Who we are (Data Fiduciary)

Drut is a multi-exam, self-study competitive-exam preparation tool. It offers a question bank of original, AI-generated and human-curated multiple-choice questions, with step-by-step solutions, that you practise in two modes: Practice and timed Sprints. Drut is not a coaching centre and not a teaching platform — it is a self-study practice and solutions tool. We do not host or serve copyrighted textbook PDFs.

2. Scope & what this policy covers

This policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, the legal basis on which we process it, who we share it with, how long we keep it, how we protect it, and the rights you have. It applies to everyone who creates an account on or uses the Service, including the parent or lawful guardian of any user under 18.

It does not cover third-party websites or services you may reach through links — those are governed by their own privacy policies.

3. The laws this policy follows

We have written this policy to comply with the data-protection laws currently in force in India and with the new regime being phased in:

  • The Information Technology Act, 2000 and the Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011 (the "SPDI Rules") — currently in force.
  • The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 and the Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025 (notified in the Official Gazette on 13 November 2025), whose substantive obligations are being phased in, with the core duties expected to take effect around 13 May 2027 — the same date on which the SPDI Rules are due to be repealed.

During this transition we satisfy both regimes and apply the stricter standard. We will update this policy as the DPDP Rules come fully into force.

[CONFIRM with counsel] The exact DPDP commencement date and the precise rule numbers and timeframes (for example, the rights/grievance response window and the log-retention period) should be verified against the gazetted Rules before this policy is published.

Most of our processing rests on your consent; some rests on a "legitimate use" under Section 7 of the DPDP Act (for example, processing data you voluntarily give us in order to respond to your own request); and a small amount is necessary to perform our contract with you (delivering the paid subscription you bought).

A note on "sensitive" data

Under the SPDI Rules, "sensitive personal data" includes passwords and financial information. Almost none of what we collect is sensitive: your name, email, phone, city and school details are ordinary personal information. The only sensitive items in scope are your password (stored only as a hash by our auth provider) and financial information — and we deliberately never store financial information ourselves; it is handled entirely by Razorpay (see Section 7).

5. How we collect your data

We collect personal data in the following ways:

  • Directly from you — when you sign up, fill in your profile, contact support, or buy Drut Pro.
  • Automatically as you use the Service — your practice attempts, scores, streaks and Sprint times are generated as you study; limited technical and log data is generated to run and secure the Service.
  • From Google — if you choose to sign in with Google, we receive basic profile information from Google (see Section 6).
  • From Razorpay — when you pay, Razorpay confirms the payment to us and returns a payment reference and subscription status; it does not pass us your card/UPI/bank details.

6. Google sign-in (Google OAuth)

You can create or access your Drut account using Google sign-in (Google OAuth) instead of an email-and-password login. If you choose this option:

  • Google authenticates you and shares a limited set of profile information with us — typically your name and email address (and a Google account identifier). We use this only to create and secure your account and to identify you.
  • We do not receive or store your Google password.
  • Your use of Google sign-in is also governed by Google's own privacy policy and terms. Google acts as a sign-in provider (a sub-processor) for this purpose — see Section 8.
  • You can revoke Drut's access to your Google account at any time from your Google Account settings; you can also delete your Drut account entirely (see Section 17).

7. Payments — handled solely by Razorpay

Drut Pro is a paid subscription: ₹199 / month and ₹1999 / year (₹1799 as a first-year introductory price), with 20 free questions per day on the free tier. Purchases are made on the web only (drut.club) — there is no in-app purchase in the iOS or Android apps.

All payments are processed by Razorpay, a PCI-DSS-compliant payment processor. This matters for your privacy:

  • Drut never collects, stores, or has access to your card, UPI, or bank-account details. When you pay, you enter those details directly into Razorpay's secure, hosted checkout — they never touch Drut's servers. This is in line with the Reserve Bank of India's card-data-storage rules, under which only card networks and issuing banks may store card credentials.
  • What we receive back from Razorpay is limited to a payment reference and your subscription status (for example, "payment successful, plan active") — enough to grant your Pro access and issue an invoice, but not your financial details.
  • Razorpay processes your payment information as an independent processor under its own privacy policy and PCI-DSS obligations.
[CONFIRM] Drut Pro does not auto-renew. Access lapses at the end of each billing period unless you manually re-subscribe. If automatic renewal is introduced in future, it will follow RBI e-mandate rules and you will be notified in advance. Prices are stated as inclusive of applicable taxes — confirm with your CA and show the GST break-up on the invoice.

9. Cross-border processing

Some of our processors (for example, the infrastructure behind Supabase, Google and Razorpay) may store or process data on servers located outside India. Where this happens, we rely on (a) contractual terms requiring those processors to protect your data to a standard at least equivalent to that required under Indian law, and (b) your consent and the necessity of the transfer to provide the Service you have asked for. We will not transfer your data to any country or entity to which the Government of India has restricted such transfers under the DPDP Act.

10. Consent & how to withdraw it

Where we rely on your consent, that consent is free, specific, informed, unconditional and unambiguous, and given by a clear affirmative action. We ask only for what is necessary for the purpose we describe, and we do not bundle unrelated consents or make the Service conditional on consent you do not need to give.

You can withdraw your consent at any time, and withdrawing it is as easy as giving it. You can do this from your account settings or by emailing us at support@drut.club. When you withdraw consent:

  • we will stop the processing that relied on that consent, and
  • we will erase the relevant personal data unless we are required or permitted by law to keep it (for example, tax records).

Withdrawing consent does not affect any processing we lawfully carried out before you withdrew it. Please note that some processing is essential to providing the Service — if you withdraw consent for that, we may no longer be able to provide you the account or the paid features.

11. Children & users under 18

Under Indian law, a child is anyone under the age of 18. Many students who use Drut are 16–18 years old, so this section is important.

Users under 18 may use Drut only with the verifiable consent of a parent or lawful guardian. We do not knowingly process a child's personal data without first obtaining that consent.

How we obtain and verify parental/guardian consent

Before processing a child's personal data, we obtain consent from a parent or guardian and take reasonable steps to verify that the consenting person is an identifiable adult. Depending on the method available, this verification may use:

  • reliable identity and age details already available to us;
  • identity details the parent/guardian voluntarily provides and that can be verified; or
  • a government-issued or government-authorised digital identity or age token (for example, a virtual token such as those issued through DigiLocker-style services).

We keep a record of the consent obtained.

Strict protections for children (these apply even where a parent has consented)

For users we know or reasonably believe to be under 18, we commit that we:

  • do not track or behaviourally monitor the child;
  • do not profile the child for advertising and do not direct any targeted advertising at the child;
  • do not use device-fingerprinting or cross-site behavioural tracking of the child; and
  • do not process a child's data in any way likely to cause a detrimental effect on the child's well-being.

A parent or lawful guardian may, at any time, review the child's data, withdraw consent, and request that we correct or erase the child's data, by contacting us at support@drut.club or our Grievance Officer (see Section 18).

[CONFIRM with counsel — important] The DPDP Rules contain a narrow exemption from some child-consent steps for certain "educational institutions" and limited purposes. It is genuinely unsettled whether a commercial self-study ed-tech app like Drut qualifies. We have drafted conservatively — assuming the full verifiable-parental-consent regime applies and that no behavioural tracking or targeted advertising to minors is permitted — and this position should be confirmed by Indian data-protection counsel before launch. The exact verifiable-parental-consent mechanism (for example, DigiLocker integration) should also be confirmed against final Board guidance.

12. Cookies, analytics & tracking

We use cookies and similar local-storage technologies primarily for essential purposes — keeping you logged in, maintaining your session, and securing your account (these are provided through our authentication system). These are necessary for the Service to work.

We do not use cookies or trackers for behavioural advertising or to build advertising profiles, and — as set out in Section 11 — we do not behaviourally track or serve targeted ads to users under 18.

If we use any analytics to understand, in aggregate, how the Service is used and to improve it, we will use it in a privacy-respectful way and will update this section to name the tool and its purpose. You can control or clear cookies through your browser or device settings, though disabling essential cookies may stop you from logging in.

13. Data retention & erasure

We keep your personal data only for as long as it is needed for the purposes described in this policy, or for as long as the law requires:

  • Account, profile and performance data — kept while your account is active. If you delete your account or withdraw consent, we erase or anonymise this data, except where we must keep limited records by law.
  • Subscription and billing/tax records — kept for as long as required under applicable tax and financial-record-keeping laws, even after account closure.
  • Security and access logs — retained for the period required to meet our security obligations (we retain access logs for at least the minimum period required by law).

When the purpose for which we hold a piece of personal data is no longer being served, and no law requires us to keep it, we erase it. You can ask us to erase your data at any time (see Section 16 and Section 17).

14. Security safeguards

We maintain a documented, proportionate information-security programme designed to protect your personal data, with reasonable security safeguards including:

  • Encryption of personal data in transit and at rest;
  • Hashed passwords — passwords are never stored in plain text; our auth provider (Supabase) stores them only as salted hashes;
  • Access controls — least-privilege access, so only authorised people and systems can reach personal data;
  • Logging and monitoring of access to and processing of personal data, with logs retained as required by law;
  • Backups and recovery procedures;
  • Contractual security obligations imposed on our processors (Supabase, Google, Razorpay), and reliance on their own certifications — notably Razorpay's PCI-DSS compliance for payments.

No method of transmission or storage is ever completely secure, but we work continuously to protect your data and improve our safeguards. If you believe your account has been compromised, contact us immediately at support@drut.club.

15. Personal-data-breach handling

If a personal-data breach occurs, we will treat it as a priority. In line with the DPDP Act we will:

  • notify each affected user without delay, describing the nature of the breach, its likely consequences, the measures we have taken or propose to take to mitigate it, and the steps you can take to protect yourself; and
  • notify the Data Protection Board of India without delay, followed by a detailed report to the Board within the prescribed period (reported as 72 hours, extendable on request).

16. Your rights as a Data Principal

As a Data Principal under the DPDP Act, you have the following rights. To exercise any of them, email support@drut.club or our Grievance Officer (see Section 18); you can also use the in-app and account controls where available. We will respond within the timeframe required by law.

  • Right to access — to obtain a summary of the personal data we process about you and how we process it.
  • Right to correction, completion and updating — to have inaccurate or incomplete data corrected, completed or updated.
  • Right to erasure — to have your personal data erased when it is no longer needed for the purpose it was collected, or when you withdraw consent, unless we are required by law to keep it.
  • Right to withdraw consent — as easy to do as it was to give (see Section 10).
  • Right to grievance redressal — to a readily available channel to raise concerns (see Section 18).
  • Right to nominate — to nominate another person to exercise your rights on your behalf in the event of your death or incapacity.

Under the SPDI Rules (while in force) you also have the right to review and correct your information, an option not to provide data, and the option to withdraw consent at any time — on withdrawal we may decline to provide the service for which the data was needed.

17. Account & data deletion

You can delete your Drut account and the personal data associated with it at any time. We offer:

  • an in-app account-deletion option within the iOS and Android apps, and an equivalent option in your account settings on drut.club; and
  • a web-based deletion request you can use even if you have uninstalled the app, by emailing support@drut.club from the address on your account.

When you delete your account, we erase or anonymise your personal data, except for the limited records we are required to keep by law (such as tax and billing records) or that we may retain for security, fraud-prevention or legal-compliance purposes, as disclosed in this policy. If you signed in with Google, deleting your Drut account does not delete your Google account — you can separately revoke Drut's access from your Google Account settings.
