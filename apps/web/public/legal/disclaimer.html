1. What Drut Is (and Is Not)

Drut is a multi-exam, self-study practice and solutions tool. It provides a question bank of original, multiple-choice questions (offered as Practice and timed Sprints) with step-by-step solutions, to help students prepare for competitive entrance examinations. Its first supported exam is EAPCET (the Andhra Pradesh and Telangana engineering / agriculture / pharmacy entrance examination).

Drut is NOT:

  • a coaching centre or a tuition / teaching institution;

  • a guarantor of any examination outcome; or
  • a source of official notifications, syllabi, results, counselling, or admissions information.

Drut is a tool you use to practise and self-study. Your results depend on your own effort, preparation, and circumstances.

3. AI-Assisted and AI-Generated Content — Verify Against Official Sources

A significant portion of Drut's questions and solutions is generated with the assistance of artificial intelligence (AI) and then curated by humans. AI systems are powerful but imperfect. AI-assisted or AI-generated material may contain factual errors, incorrect answers, flawed reasoning, outdated information, or content that does not match the current official syllabus or exam pattern, even after human review.

Accordingly:

  • You should treat all Content as study aid material, not as a verified or authoritative answer key.
  • Always verify important facts, formulas, definitions, answers, and exam-specific information against official sources — including the official notification, syllabus, and prescribed textbooks of the relevant examination authority — before relying on them for your exam.
  • Drut is not liable for any loss, lower score, missed concept, or other consequence arising from an error in AI-assisted or AI-generated Content, to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law and subject to your consumer rights and our Terms of Service.

If you believe a question or solution is wrong, please report it to us (see Section 12) so we can review and correct it.

In plain English We build many questions with AI and then have people check them — but AI can still get things wrong. Use Drut to practise, and always confirm anything that matters against the official syllabus and prescribed books.

Many users of the Service are between 16 and 18 years old. Under Indian law, anyone under 18 years of age is a child. Users under 18 may use Drut only with the verifiable consent of a parent or lawful guardian. If you are under 18, you confirm that your parent or guardian has reviewed and agreed to this Disclaimer, our Terms of Service, and our Privacy Policy on your behalf.

The disclaimers in this document — including that Content is provided "as is", that AI-assisted content may contain errors, and that no exam outcome is guaranteed — apply equally to students under 18 and to their parents/guardians. We encourage parents and guardians to supervise their child's use of the Service and to help verify important Content against official sources.

In line with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, Drut does not track, behaviourally monitor, profile, or serve targeted advertising to children, and does not process children's data in a way likely to cause a detrimental effect on a child's well-being. How we handle children's data and obtain verifiable parental consent is described in our Privacy Policy.

Business decision — confirm with counsel Drut's position is that under-18 users are permitted with verifiable parental/guardian consent (not an "18+ only" service).
