    $F_1 = \frac{m_1 v_1^2}{R_1} = \frac{m v^2}{R}$

    2. Calculate the centripetal force for the second body ($F_2$):
    $F_2 = \frac{m_2 v_2^2}{R_2} = \frac{(2m) (2v)^2}{(R/2)}$
    $F_2 = \frac{2m (4v^2)}{R/2} = \frac{8mv^2}{R/2}$
    To simplify, multiply the numerator by the reciprocal of the denominator:
    $F_2 = 8mv^2 \times \frac{2}{R} = \frac{16mv^2}{R}$

    3. Calculate the ratio of the centripetal force for the second body to the first body ($F_2/F_1$):
    $\frac{F_2}{F_1} = \frac{\frac{16mv^2}{R}}{\frac{mv^2}{R}}$
    $\frac{F_2}{F_1} = 16$
  4. PROOF: The calculation is straightforward. The force is directly proportional to mass and the square of speed, and inversely proportional to the radius. Doubling the mass doubles the force (factor of 2). Doubling the speed quadruples the force ($2^2=4$, so a factor of 4). Halving the radius doubles the force (factor of 2). Combining these factors: $2 \times 4 \times 2 = 16$. The result is consistent.
