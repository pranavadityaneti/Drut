ExPhysics · Motion in a Plane · Projectile rangeMedium
A ball is projected at $u=20\,\text{m/s}$ at $30^\circ$ above the horizontal. Taking $g=10\,\text{m/s}^2$ and $\sqrt3\approx1.73$, find its horizontal range (launch and landing at the same height).
  1. A$20\,\text{m}$
  2. B$20\sqrt3\,\text{m}\approx34.6\,\text{m}$✓
  3. C$40\,\text{m}$
  4. D$17.3\,\text{m}$
⚡ Quick Method
  1. Same-height range is $R=\dfrac{u^2\sin2\theta}{g}$.
  2. Here $2\theta=60^\circ$, $\sin60^\circ=\frac{\sqrt3}{2}$: $\;R=\dfrac{400\cdot0.866}{10}$.
  3. $R\approx34.6\,\text{m}=20\sqrt3\,\text{m}$ → option B.
🔬 Full Solution
For a projectile launched and landing at the same height, the horizontal range is set entirely by the launch speed and angle through $R=\dfrac{u^2\sin2\theta}{g}$.
The range for equal launch/landing height is
$$R=\frac{u^2\sin 2\theta}{g}.$$
With $u=20\,\text{m/s}$ and $\theta=30^\circ$, the doubled angle is $2\theta=60^\circ$, and $\sin60^\circ=\tfrac{\sqrt3}{2}\approx0.866$.
Substituting these values,
$$R=\frac{(20)^2\,(0.866)}{10}=\frac{400\times0.866}{10}=34.6\,\text{m}.$$
So the range is $20\sqrt3\,\text{m}$, about $34.6\,\text{m}$.
Answer: $20\sqrt3\,\text{m}\approx34.6\,\text{m}$ — option B.
Worked examples — same method, new numbers
1. A stone is thrown at $30\,\text{m/s}$ at $45^\circ$ ($g=10\,\text{m/s}^2$). Find its range.
At $45^\circ$ the doubled angle is $90^\circ$ and $\sin90^\circ=1$, so the range is maximal for the speed:
$$R=\frac{(30)^2\times1}{10}=\frac{900}{10}=90\,\text{m}.$$
Range $=90\,\text{m}$.
2. Two balls are thrown with equal speed at $20^\circ$ and $70^\circ$. Why are their ranges equal?
Range depends on $\sin2\theta$. For $20^\circ$, $2\theta=40^\circ$; for $70^\circ$, $2\theta=140^\circ$. Since $\sin140^\circ=\sin(180^\circ-140^\circ)=\sin40^\circ$, both share the same $\sin2\theta$.
Equal ranges — complementary launch angles ($20^\circ+70^\circ=90^\circ$) always match.
